Join a dynamic community shaping the future of work!

Each spring, the Project on Workforce at Harvard brings together a select group of students from across Harvard schools to explore critical issues at the intersection of education, business, technology, and labor market policy. Participants engage in interactive sessions and gain insights from leaders across business, public policy, government, technology, and education. It’s a unique opportunity to connect with peers and professors deeply engaged in these complex, cross-sector challenges. This study group gives you access to a broader network, with alumni who have gone on to lead in top organizations, found cutting-edge companies, and drive innovations in the workforce. Selection into the study group is made through a competitive application process. The application will open in early December. Sign up below to get more information on the programming and to be notified when the application opens.

Building The Evidence Base For The Future Of Work

1

Social Capital in the Age of AI

Join Kerry McKittrick, Co-Director of the Project on Workforce, and Nitzan Pelman, CEO and Founder of Climb Together, for a discussion about the critical role of social capital in economic mobility and the emerging opportunities to leverage generative AI to increase equity and access to social capital

2

Healthcare Workforce Solutions

oin Chike Aguh, Senior Advisor to the Project on Workforce and former Chief Innovation Officer at the U.S. Department of Labor, and Geoffrey Roche, Director of Workforce Development at Siemens Healthineers, for a discussion about new approaches to addressing workforce inequalities and scaling innovative training models in healthcare.

3

AI and the Future of Skills

Join Nathalie Gazzaneo, Co-Director of the Project on Workforce, and Ben Weidmann, Director of Research at the Harvard Skills Lab, for a discussion about the challenges of creating scalable measures of teamwork and management skills, and how new research on AI agents can help.

4

Microcredentials in Focus

Join Kathleen deLaski, Senior Advisor to the Project on Workforce and Founder and Board Chair at Education Design Lab, and Michelle Van Noy, Director of the Education and Employment Research Center at Rutgers University, for a discussion about micro-credentials and their impact on the education and work ecosystem.
